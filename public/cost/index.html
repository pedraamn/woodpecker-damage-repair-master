Woodpecker Damage Repair Cost

Typical pricing ranges, scope examples, and what drives the total for siding and trim repairs.

How Much Does Woodpecker Damage Repair Cost?

The cost of woodpecker damage repair generally ranges from $300 to $800, based on the number of damaged areas and how spread out they are. Local labor rates and exterior finish requirements play a role in the total cost.

Woodpecker Damage Repair Cost Ranges (Most Common Repairs)

Repair Scenario Typical Cost Range What You’re Paying For
Small hole repair (up to ~2") $150–$350 Clearing out debris, epoxy patching, sealing, and touch-ups
Medium hole repair (~2–6") $300–$800 Plugging deep holes, weather sealing, and finish blending
Large hole repair (over ~6") $600–$1,500 Area reconstruction or partial replacement, sealing, and finish
Replace a damaged siding board / small area $500–$2,500 Removing damaged material, moisture protection, and finish matching
Structural repair (sheathing/stud/insulation affected) $1,000–$3,500+ Wall access, replacing damaged wood, and restoring weather barriers
Interior wall repair (if penetrated) $250–$900 Drywall repair, matching the texture, and repainting
Paint/stain blending (separate line item) $150–$600 Priming and custom blending to mask the repair
High access work (2nd story / steep roofline) +15% to +50% Preparation time, safety gear, and lift or ladder setup

Typical total: $300–$2,500. When hidden damage is present: $5,000+ is possible.

Cost by Severity (Fast Self-Assessment)

Minor

  • What it looks like: 1–2 small holes, shallow pecks, solid wood
  • Expected cost: $150–$500
  • Common repair: epoxy patch/plug + seal + spot finish

Moderate

  • What it looks like: multiple holes in one zone, repeated pecking on the same board
  • Expected cost: $500–$2,500
  • Common repair: multi-hole patching or board/panel replacement + finish blending

Severe

  • What it looks like: cavity access, soft/rotted wood, water staining, nesting attempts
  • Expected cost: $2,500–$5,000+
  • Common repair: open-up + structural repair + insulation/water barrier restoration

What Increases Woodpecker Repair Costs

  • Hidden moisture: rot, swelling, or wet sheathing located behind siding
  • Hole depth: when holes reach the cavity or insulation, costs rise
  • Repeat targeting: many boards or corners needing repair and protection
  • Access: high peaks, chimneys, steep rooflines, or difficult grades
  • Finish matching: old stain/paint requires professional blending

When Patching Is Enough vs When Replacement Is Required

Patching is usually enough if:

  • The wood is hard when probed
  • Holes are shallow and limited to the surface
  • No water staining, softness, or swelling is present

Replacement is usually required if:

  • The wood feels soft or spongy
  • Holes are large, deep, or connected internally
  • There is rot, cracking, swelling, or delamination
  • The same board has been hit repeatedly

Rule: If a screwdriver sinks in easily, replacement is more reliable than patching.

Prevention Costs (Avoid Paying Twice)

Repairing damage alone often leads to new holes. Physical barriers are what stop birds for good.

Prevention Method Typical Cost Best Use
Bird netting over the target area $150–$800 Areas on siding walls with repeated pecking
Hardware cloth / barrier panels $150–$900 Vulnerable corners, fascia, and trim boards
Metal flashing / corner protection $200–$1,000 Exposed edges and high roofline sections
Professional wildlife exclusion / control $300–$1,500 Persistent bird activity or nesting habits
Visual deterrents (tape/decoys) $20–$150 Temporary aid only (not a permanent solution)

What a Siding Repair Quote Should Include

  • Hole count and largest hole diameter
  • Patch vs board/panel replacement scope
  • Waterproofing plan (sealant, flashing, weather barrier restoration)
  • Finish plan (prime + paint blend or stain match)
  • Access plan (ladder vs lift and how it impacts price)
  • Prevention plan (netting/barriers) to reduce repeat damage

Woodpecker Damage Insurance Coverage (Common Reality)

Homeowners insurance might cover woodpecker damage depending on specific policy language. Coverage is more likely for sudden damage rather than slow decay. Take photos of the damage immediately and verify if animal-related repairs are covered.
